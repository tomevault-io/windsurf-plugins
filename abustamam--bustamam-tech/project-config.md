# Hero Image Guidelines

The hero image should be an **action shot** showing you in your element - coding, presenting, or consulting. This is more authentic and engaging than a generic headshot for a consulting practice.

## Image Concepts (Choose One)

1. **Coding/Development**: You at your computer, coding, with code visible on screen
2. **Presenting/Consulting**: You presenting to clients, whiteboarding, or in a consultation setting
3. **Collaboration**: Working with a team or client (if you have permission to use)

## What It Should Convey

- **Authenticity**: Real work, real expertise
- **Approachability**: You're accessible and collaborative
- **Technical competence**: Shows you know what you're doing
- **Professionalism**: Still polished and professional, not overly casual

## Technical Specifications

- **Dimensions**:
  - Landscape: Minimum 1200x800px (3:2 or 16:10 aspect ratio)
  - Portrait: Minimum 800x1200px (2:3 aspect ratio)
  - Current layout works best with portrait orientation (4:5 aspect ratio)
- **Format**: JPG or WebP (optimized for web)
- **File size**: Under 500KB for fast loading
- **Resolution**: High quality, sharp and clear
- **Lighting**: Well-lit, natural lighting preferred
- **Composition**: You should be the clear focal point, but context (screen, whiteboard, etc.) should be visible

## Style Recommendations

- **Natural setting**: Real workspace, not overly staged
- **Good lighting**: Natural light or well-lit workspace
- **Visible context**: Code on screen, presentation materials, or consultation setting should be visible but not distracting
- **You in focus**: You should be clearly visible and well-composed
- **Professional but authentic**: Business casual to professional attire, but natural pose
- **Color harmony**: Should work well with the site's color scheme (light/dark mode compatible)

## What to Avoid

- Overly staged or stock-photo-like images
- Poor lighting or blurry shots
- Too much clutter or distracting backgrounds
- Overly casual (tank tops, messy workspace)
- Generic headshots without context

## Placement

- Image should be placed in `/public/images/hero-action.jpg` (or update path in code)
- Update the `src` attribute in `src/routes/index.tsx` when ready
- Consider using a tool like TinyPNG or Squoosh to optimize the image before uploading

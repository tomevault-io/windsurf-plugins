# CLAUDE.md — Anarchy In America

## Project Overview

This is an alternate history worldbuilding and fiction project. The core work is:

1. **An alternate history setting** in which the United States developed as a stateless kritarchy rather than a democratic republic
2. **A hardboiled detective novel** set in the modern day of that setting, featuring investigator Farley Tiergarten
3. **A fictional counterpart of Tocqueville's *Democracy In America***, titled *Anarchy In America*, which serves as both worldbuilding artifact and plot device (a stolen first edition is the MacGuffin)

---

## Project Phase

The project has completed its initial worldbuilding foundation and is transitioning toward story development — blocking out scenes, deepening characters, and developing the narrative structure — with worldbuilding continuing as needed to support the narrative.

**What's been built:**
- A detailed alternate history timeline from the 1787 divergence through the modern era
- A structural analysis of how the kritarchy works as a living society — its institutional architecture, incentive structures, cultural landscape, and relationship to the wider world
- Core characters sketched with varying depth, from well-developed (Farley, the sifu, the sihing, Denton, the overseas buyer/Hamburg collector) to placeholder (the instigator, the insurance company contact, the Canadian cop)
- International relations framework: how the Hansa interfaces with foreign governments, Canadian border mechanics, Canadian attitudes, Mexico/Latin America dynamics, European intellectual engagement with the kritarchy and the Hanseatic parallel
- Vancouver/Victoria established as the novel's Canadian setting, with specific plot logic for why the book goes north rather than south
- The plot structure in broad strokes, with key beats identified but not yet blocked into scenes
- Thematic questions identified and articulated

**What comes next:**
- Filling in recent history (1950s–present) with enough specificity to generate the texture and mood of Farley's daily world
- Developing underdeveloped characters — particularly the instigator, the logistics sibling, the insurance company contact, and the Canadian cop
- Blocking out the story scene by scene
- Developing San Francisco's specific history and geography as a setting
- Drafting sample Tocqueville epigraphs to nail the voice
- Deepening the sihing's financial discovery — what is the specific irregularity, what does it connect to?

## Reference Documents

The project is organized into four documents with distinct purposes:

### `CLAUDE.md` (this file)
**Purpose:** Project guide. How to work on this, not what the work contains. Read this first to orient.

### `history-outline.md`
**Purpose:** The alternate history timeline — what happened and when, from the 1787 divergence forward. Organized chronologically. Captures the logic of how events unfold from the point of divergence, with the guiding principle that we follow plausible historical logic in the early period and lean more on teleology (steering toward what the story needs) as we move into the less-anchored later periods.

### `the-modern-kritarchy.md`
**Purpose:** Structural analysis of how the kritarchy works as a living society. Organized thematically, not chronologically. Covers institutional architecture (insurance ecosystem, security, judiciary, common law), technology, culture, infrastructure, and daily life. The companion to the history outline: the timeline tells you how the kritarchy got here; this document tells you what "here" looks like from the inside.

### `story-outline.md`
**Purpose:** The novel itself — plot structure, characters, themes, important beats, the fictional *Anarchy in America* and its use in the narrative, and open questions specific to the story. This is the document that will be worked from most actively as the project moves into the story-development phase.

## Role and Approach

Claude is acting as a **creative collaborator and brainstorming partner**. The author (Mikel) has deep knowledge in several areas relevant to the project, including Chinese martial arts (studied under Chen Xiaowang for a decade), voluntary legal systems, libertarian political philosophy, and the San Francisco Bay Area (a decade of residence). He writes by immersing himself in characters and settings and daydreaming events into being, so the goal of collaboration is to build out the world and characters thoroughly enough to support that process.

### Working Method

- **Explore implications.** The best worldbuilding discoveries come from pushing on an idea and following where the logic leads. A question about insurance companies led to discovering that the kritarchy's entire institutional architecture functions like the internet — voluntary participation disciplined by the credible threat of withdrawal.
- **Build incrementally.** Mikel works by chewing on chunks of manageable size, thinking carefully about each piece before moving on. Don't try to resolve everything at once.

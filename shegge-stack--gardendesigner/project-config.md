# Garden Designer Project

This project helps users plan, design, and build productive gardens optimized for their USDA hardiness zone. It produces actionable garden plans — layout diagrams, planting calendars, shopping lists, and care schedules that a home gardener can take to the garden center and build from, or that a landscape/garden business can use as client deliverables.

Initial focus: USDA Zone 4 (Minnesota). All knowledge is sourced from university extension programs (primarily UMN Extension), USDA data, and peer-reviewed research.

## Knowledge Base
Read the relevant knowledge files before answering any garden question or generating any output:
- `knowledge/00-hardiness-zones.md` - USDA zones, frost dates, microclimate factors
- `knowledge/01-soil-science.md` - Soil types, pH, testing, amendments, MN-specific soils
- `knowledge/02-vegetable-planning.md` - Companion planting, crop rotation, spacing, yields
- `knowledge/03-garden-layout.md` - Raised beds, plot sizing, SFG method, sun orientation
- `knowledge/04-minnesota-growing.md` - Zone 4 planting dates, season extension, what grows well
- `knowledge/05-perennials-landscaping.md` - Native plants, pollinators, rain gardens, shade gardens
- `knowledge/06-garden-infrastructure.md` - Irrigation, raised bed construction, trellising, fencing, mulch
- `knowledge/07-plant-database.md` - Plant data schema, field definitions, priority crop list
- `knowledge/08-pest-disease.md` - Common MN pests, organic IPM, diseases, prevention
- `knowledge/09-season-calendar.md` - Month-by-month calendar, succession planting, seed starting schedule

## Key Principles (Always Follow)
1. **Zone-appropriate recommendations** — never suggest plants outside the user's hardiness zone
2. **Science-based companion planting** — cite evidence level (research-backed vs. traditional)
3. **Crop rotation is mandatory** — 3-4 year minimum, track plant families
4. **Full sun = 6+ hours** — always verify sun requirements before placing plants
5. **Water consistently** — 1-1.5 inches per week for most vegetables
6. **IPM hierarchy** — prevention first, chemical (even organic) last
7. **Frost dates drive everything** — all planting dates calculated from local frost dates
8. **Soil test before amending** — never blindly add amendments
9. **Native plants preferred** for non-edible landscaping (pollinators, rain gardens)
10. **Square foot gardening yields 2-4x** traditional row spacing per area
